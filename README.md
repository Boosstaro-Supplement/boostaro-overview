Research on individual ingredients is not the same as clinical evidence on the finished Boostaro formula. A careful review should identify what type of claim is being made and what evidence supports it.

| Claim Type | What It Means | What To Check |
|---|---|---|
| Ingredient study | Research discusses one ingredient or pathway | Dose, population, study design, and whether the finished product was studied |
| Traditional use | An ingredient has historical or general wellness use | Whether modern safety and dose information is available |
| Marketing claim | A page summarizes expected benefits | Whether the language avoids disease-treatment claims and certain-result promises |
| Finished-product evidence | Research tests the complete formula | Whether the study is product-specific, well-designed, and relevant |
| Customer review | A user reports a personal experience | Whether the review is authentic, balanced, and not used as proof for every user |
